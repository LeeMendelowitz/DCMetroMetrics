About DC Metro Metrics

Tracking escalator outages, elevator breakdowns, and #wmata #hotcar's since 2013.

What?

DC Metro Metrics is website that tracks escalator outages, elevator outages, and rider #hotcar reports in the WMATA Metrorail system. We have the performance history for every escalator and elevator since the summer of 2013. See the rankings page for the best and worst performing escalators and elevators in the system.

Why?

Escalator outages are often cited again and again as one of Metrorail's most prevalent problems.

When I first learned that WMATA had an open data feed listing the current escalator outages, I initially decided to build a Twitter bot (@MetroEscalators, launched March 2013). It makes for an interesting type of data visualization, but isn't all that useful. But it did get some love from the dcist.

I realized it would be possible to take this a step further and compile the performance history for every escalator in the system. This is a unique dataset that helps us objectively answer questions about escalator reliability that we couldn't answer previously.

I started tracking #wmata #hotcar's in the summer of 2013. The #hotcar thing got started in summer 2011 thanks to @FixWMATA (now @FixMetro). He decided he didn't want to track the reports anymore, so I decided to automate the process of collecting the reports.

How?

We source all of our escalator and elevator data in realtime from the WMATA API, and #hotcar reports from riders over the Twitter API.

The back end is implemented with Python and MongoDB and deployed on DigitalOcean. The front end is implemented using AngularJS. D3.js is used for the hotcar visualization, cal-heatmap is used for the calendar visualization. The project is open source under GPLv2 and available on GitHub. Contributions are welcome!
